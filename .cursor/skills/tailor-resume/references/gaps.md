# Gaps output schema

Write `gaps.md` in plain language. Use the tier only as a quick orientation label, then make the verdict and next step understandable without jargon.

Required sections:

## Bottom line

Required format:

```md
**Tier:** Strong | Moderate | Stretch
**Verdict:** <plain-language verdict from the table below>

2-3 sentences explaining whether the role is close, adjacent, or a reach; why; and what the candidate should do next.
```

| Tier | Required verdict line | Recommended next step |
|------|-----------------------|-----------------------|
| **Strong** | `**Verdict:** Good fit - apply with the tailored resume` | Apply with the tailored resume. |
| **Moderate** | `**Verdict:** Reasonable fit with gaps - review gaps before applying` | Apply only if the candidate can honestly speak to the gaps. |
| **Stretch** | `**Verdict:** Too far a stretch for this role - try closer-aligned roles` | Prefer roles aligned closer to the skills already supported by `resume.md` instead of forcing this application. |

Do not soften a Stretch verdict by inventing unsupported experience. The sections below should explain honest matches, missing proof, bridges, and do-not-claim items.

## What lines up

Bullets or a small table connecting what the job asks for to evidence in `resume.md`.

## What is missing or not proven

Skills or themes the job asks for that are not shown clearly in `resume.md`. Be direct and calm.

## Helpful bridges

Optional. Transferable-skill connections from `resume.md` that do not directly satisfy a job requirement but can support the cover letter or suggestions.

## Do not claim

List job requirements with **no** support in `resume.md`. These must not appear in `tailored-resume.md`.

Optional: quick scan checklist for title, core stack, relevant tools, and years of experience.
